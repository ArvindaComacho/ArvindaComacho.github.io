Renewable energy comes from unlimited, naturally replenished resources like the sun, water, and wind. It is important as it can generate large amounts of electricity. This electricity can provide space and water heating, cooling, and transportation to name a few.

In contrast, non-renewable energy comes from limited sources such as coal, natural gas, and oil which are generally referred to as fossil fuels.

Renewable energy systems work by converting natural resources like sunlight, wind, or water into usable forms of energy, such as electricity or heat. Solar panels, for instance, capture sunlight and transform it into electricity using photovoltaic cells.

Wind turbines convert wind movement into mechanical energy, which is then turned into electricity. These systems are designed to be sustainable and environmentally friendly.
