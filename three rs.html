The 3Rs of waste management are rules that involve reducing, reusing, and recycling waste to help conserve resources and energy and keep landfills and waterways from overflowing.

Garbage is disposed of in 3 main ways namely composting, landfill dumping, and incineration. While they can be beneficial to the community, the latter methods are often executed improperly, by even our own local garbage collectors. These trends directly influence land pollution, water pollution and of course, air pollution. See below to learn about the 3 methods of waste disposal.

Reduce: Reduce means to make smaller/less in amount; use things with care to create less waste. For example, you can use a reusable lunch bag instead of a plastic bag or use reusable containers instead of plastic baggies.

Reuse: Find new ways to use items that would otherwise be thrown away. Reuse means to use something again, either for its original purpose or repurposed for a different task. For example, containers can be reused at home or for school projects. Also, wrapping paper, plastic bags and lumber can all be reused. Clothing that has been outgrown can be donated to charity or friends.

Recycle: Recycle means to convert waste into material that can be used to remake the item or to make something else. For example, you can recycle plastic milk jugs to make picnic benches or playground equipment. THere are separate rubbish bins that are meant for recyclable items such as glass, plastic and metals too!

You may have noticed that when referring to the 3Rs, we always say them in the same order: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle. This is because this is the order that is most effective in helping us to reduce waste and greenhouse gas emissions.

  • Make sure your waste is clean before recycling it.
  • Composting is a free and sustainable way to dispose of organic waste, like food scraps, and create nutrient-rich soil for gardening.
  • Reuse glass jars for storage.
  • Have reusable drink bottles and cups.
  • Avoid using plastic bags, packing material, and other plastic items.
  • Donate old belongings to people in need
  • Shop local farmers markets and buy in bulk to reduce packaging
